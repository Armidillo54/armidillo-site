VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) are an important layer of security for cryptocurrency traders — especially those using exchanges on public networks or who want to protect their IP address from being linked to large transactions.

Why Crypto Traders Use VPNs

  • Public Wi-Fi protection: Encrypts your traffic when using coffee shops, airports, or hotels.
  • IP address privacy: Prevents your location from being linked to exchange activity.
  • Geo-restrictions: Access exchanges that may be restricted in your region.
  • ISP monitoring: Prevents your internet provider from seeing your crypto exchange traffic.

What to Look for in a Crypto VPN

  • No-logs policy (verified by independent audit).
  • Kill switch — cuts internet if VPN drops, preventing IP exposure.
  • Strong encryption (AES-256).
  • Fast connection speeds (critical for trading platforms).
  • No-logs policy audited by independent third party.

Important Limitations

VPNs improve privacy but do not make you anonymous. They do not protect against phishing, malware, or exchange hacks. A VPN is one layer of security — not a complete solution. Always use hardware wallets for significant holdings and never leave large amounts on exchanges.
