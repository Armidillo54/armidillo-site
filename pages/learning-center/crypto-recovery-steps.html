Crypto Recovery? Do These 6 Steps Now (U.S. & Canada) — No $10k Trace Reports

By Armidillo Team · Updated March 24, 2026

Important: Recovery Scams Are Everywhere

⚠️ Warning Before You Search for Help

The internet is flooded with fake "crypto recovery experts" who charge $5,000–$10,000 upfront for "trace reports" that do nothing. If you were just scammed, you are already on lists being sold to these secondary scammers. Any service charging upfront fees to recover crypto is itself a scam. The only legitimate steps are listed below.

The 6 Legitimate Steps

Step 1: Document Everything Immediately

Screenshot all communications, wallet addresses, transaction IDs (TXIDs), platform URLs, and profile photos. Note the scammer's contact information and the dates of all interactions. This documentation is required for every subsequent step.

Step 2: Contact Your Exchange

If you sent crypto from a centralized exchange like Coinbase, Kraken, or Crypto.com, contact their fraud team immediately. Some exchanges can freeze accounts on the receiving end if fraud is reported quickly. Include your TXIDs in the report.

Step 3: File Reports

United States:

  • FBI IC3: ic3.gov — required for any federal case
  • FTC: ReportFraud.ftc.gov
  • CFTC (if promoted as a commodity): cftc.gov/complaint
  • SEC (if promoted as a security): sec.gov/tcr

Canada:

  • Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre: 1-888-495-8501 or antifraudcentre.ca
  • Local police (for a report number)

Step 4: Consult a Blockchain Analytics Firm (If Loss Exceeds $50K)

Legitimate blockchain analytics firms like Chainalysis, CipherTrace, and Crystal Blockchain can trace crypto through the blockchain. They work with law enforcement. This is not cheap — costs $5,000+ — and only viable for large losses. Do not pay any firm that cold-contacts you offering this service.

Step 5: Consult a Lawyer

If substantial funds were lost, a lawyer specializing in crypto fraud may be able to assist. Some law firms take cases on contingency. Search for "crypto fraud attorney" in your jurisdiction.

Step 6: Protect Your Remaining Assets

Change all passwords. Enable 2FA on all accounts. Place a credit freeze. Consider enrolling in identity monitoring — your personal data may have been compromised alongside the financial fraud. If you provided ID documents to the platform, treat your identity as compromised.
