Pig Butchering Scams: From Chinese Boiler Rooms to Global Criminal Empires (2025)

By Armidillo Team · Updated March 24, 2026

What Is Pig Butchering?

Pig butchering (sha zhu pan in Mandarin) refers to the practice of "fattening the pig before slaughter" — building a trusting relationship with a victim before extracting as much money as possible. These are not simple scams; they are sophisticated, industrial-scale operations run by organized criminal enterprises.

The Criminal Infrastructure

Scam Compounds in Southeast Asia

Pig butchering operations are primarily run from compounds in Myanmar, Cambodia, and Laos — often staffed by trafficked workers forced to scam under threat of violence. The DOJ has identified and sanctioned dozens of these operations.

How the Romance Phase Works

The scammer contacts you "by mistake" — a wrong number text or a random LinkedIn/Instagram connection. They build rapport over weeks or months, often posing as a successful professional. Once trust is established, they introduce a "can't miss" crypto investment opportunity.

The Fake Platform

Victims are directed to a fraudulent trading platform that looks legitimate. Early "profits" are visible on screen and small withdrawals are permitted (to build trust). Once large sums are deposited, the platform becomes inaccessible. Victims are then told they need to pay "taxes" or "fees" to withdraw — a final extraction attempt before contact ceases.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Unsolicited contact from a stranger on any platform — text, social media, dating apps
  • They claim to be wealthy and successful but are talking to you, a stranger
  • Investment opportunities are introduced casually, as if doing you a favour
  • The trading platform is not listed on Google Play Store or Apple App Store
  • Profits are visible but require fees to withdraw
  • They become romantic quickly and suggest meeting "after you make some money together"
