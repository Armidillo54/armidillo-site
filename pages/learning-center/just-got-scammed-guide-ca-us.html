Just Got Scammed? Do These Steps Now (Canada & U.S.)

By Armidillo Team · Updated March 24, 2026

Act Immediately — Time Is Critical

If you were scammed today, the next few hours are the most important. The sooner you act, the better your chances of limiting the damage. Follow these steps in order.

Step 1: Stop All Contact

Block the scammer immediately on all platforms. Do not respond to any further contact — even if they claim they can return your money. Any continued contact is an attempt to extract more money or information.

Step 2: Contact Your Bank or Credit Card Company Right Now

Call the number on the back of your card. Report the fraud and ask to reverse any charges. Banks have fraud teams available 24/7. The sooner you call, the better your chargeback chances. If you sent a wire transfer, ask about a wire recall — it must happen within hours.

Step 3: Report to the Appropriate Authorities

In the United States:

  • FTC: ReportFraud.ftc.gov
  • FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center: IC3.gov
  • Your state attorney general's consumer protection division

In Canada:

  • Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre: 1-888-495-8501 or antifraudcentre.ca
  • Local police for a report number (required for bank claims)

Step 4: Protect Your Identity

Place a fraud alert at Equifax, TransUnion, and Experian (US) or Equifax and TransUnion (Canada). Consider a credit freeze to prevent new accounts from being opened. Change passwords for any accounts you gave the scammer access to, starting with email and banking.

Step 5: Preserve All Evidence

Screenshot all communications. Save receipts for any payments made. Note the scammer's username, phone number, and any website URLs. This evidence is required for police reports and bank fraud claims.

Step 6: Watch for Recovery Scams

After being scammed, you may be targeted again by "recovery agents" who claim they can get your money back for an upfront fee. This is always a scam. No legitimate service charges fees upfront to recover fraud losses. Your bank and law enforcement are your only legitimate options.
