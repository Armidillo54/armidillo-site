How to Report Identity Theft

Complete step-by-step guide to reporting identity theft — credit freeze, FTC report, police report, and recovery services. Act fast to minimize damage.

Your Immediate Action Plan

Act quickly — the sooner you report, the less damage can be done. Take these steps in order.

  1. Place a Fraud Alert and Freeze Your Credit

    Contact any one of the three bureaus to place a fraud alert — they're required to notify the other two. Then place a credit freeze with all three: Equifax (1-800-685-1111), Experian (1-888-397-3742), TransUnion (1-888-909-8872). Both are free.

  2. Report the Theft to the FTC

    Go to IdentityTheft.gov and file a report. The site generates a personalized recovery plan and creates an official Identity Theft Report you'll need for disputed accounts.

  3. Contact Your Financial Institutions

    Call the fraud department of your bank, credit card companies, and any other financial institutions. Report unauthorized transactions. Change passwords, enable 2FA, and request new account numbers if necessary.

  4. File a Report with Local Police

    Request a copy of the police report — creditors often require it when disputing fraudulent accounts. Bring your FTC Identity Theft Report, government-issued ID, and documentation of any fraudulent activity.

  5. Get Expert Recovery Help

    If the theft is extensive, professional restoration services save significant time and stress. Aura provides 24/7 US-based restoration specialists who handle disputes on your behalf — a critical service for anyone managing a serious identity theft case.

Keep Records of Everything: Document every call — date, time, name of representative, and what was discussed. Keep copies of all letters, forms, and emails. This paper trail is essential for disputes.
