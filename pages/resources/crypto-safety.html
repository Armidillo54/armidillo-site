Cryptocurrency Safety Guide

How to buy, store, and trade cryptocurrency securely in 2026 — avoiding the scams that cost people $5.6 billion in 2024 alone.

Quick Start: Two Things That Matter Most

  1. Never share your seed phrase or private key with anyone, ever. Not a support agent. Not a wallet app. Not someone who says they're from Coinbase. No legitimate entity will ever ask for this.
  2. Use a hardware wallet for significant holdings. If you own more than $1,000 in crypto, it belongs in cold storage — not on an exchange.

Beware of These Scams

🚨

Pig Butchering Scams: A "friend" or romantic interest introduces you to a "great" crypto investment opportunity. They help you set up a fake exchange account that shows growing profits. When you try to withdraw, you're asked to pay more "fees." There are no real profits — it's all fabricated.

⚠️

Giveaway Scams: Accounts impersonating Elon Musk, Coinbase, or major exchanges promise to double any crypto you send. They will never send anything back.
