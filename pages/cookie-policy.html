5b. Canadian Residents — PIPEDA & Quebec Law 25

Canada's PIPEDA requires meaningful consent for cookies used in commercial activity. Our cookie banner's "Essential Only" option lets you decline analytics and affiliate tracking cookies entirely, satisfying PIPEDA's consent requirements.

Quebec Residents — Explicit Opt-In Required

Quebec's Law 25 is the strictest cookie consent law in North America — equivalent to the EU's GDPR. It requires explicit opt-in consent before any tracking cookies are set. Our cookie banner is designed to satisfy this requirement:

  • No analytics or affiliate tracking cookies are set until you click "Accept All"
  • Clicking "Essential Only" blocks all non-essential cookies and disables Google Analytics
  • The default state (before you click anything) is no tracking cookies — compliant with Law 25's opt-in requirement

If you believe our cookie practices do not comply with Quebec's Law 25, you may file a complaint with the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec (CAI) at cai.gouv.qc.ca.
