Buyer's Guide: What to Look For

Resolution: 2K vs 1080p

1080p is clear enough for most uses, but 2K lets you read license plates and facial features. If you're monitoring a driveway or street-facing door, choose 2K or higher.

Battery vs. Wired

Battery-powered cameras offer flexibility — place them anywhere without wiring. Wired cameras are more reliable and never run out of power. Renters should choose battery; homeowners may prefer wired for permanent installations.

Subscription Costs

Most smart cameras require a monthly subscription to unlock cloud storage and advanced AI detection. Factor this into your total cost of ownership. The TP-Link Tapo is a notable exception with local microSD storage.

Cold Weather Performance

If you're in Canada or the northern US, verify the camera's operating temperature range. Quality cameras typically operate down to −20°C. Battery life is reduced in extreme cold — choose wired if you live in a harsh climate.

FAQ

Do I need a subscription?

Only for cloud video storage and advanced AI features. Local storage cameras (like the TP-Link Tapo) work without a subscription.

Is it legal to have security cameras?

Yes, on your own property. Avoid recording areas where people have a reasonable expectation of privacy (neighbours' yards, etc.). In Canada, notify visitors if cameras are recording audio.

Best mounting height?

Install cameras 8–10 feet high — high enough to deter tampering, low enough to capture facial details.
