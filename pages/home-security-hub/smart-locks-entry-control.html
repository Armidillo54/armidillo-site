Smart Lock Safety Tips

Monitor Battery Levels

Most smart locks run on AA batteries. Enable low-battery alerts and replace proactively — a dead battery means you're locked out or locked in.

Use Strong, Unique Passcodes

Avoid birthdays or sequential numbers. Use random 6–8 digit codes and change them regularly. Delete guest codes when they're no longer needed.

Secure Your Wi-Fi Network

Your smart lock is only as secure as your Wi-Fi. Use WPA3 encryption, a strong password, and keep your router firmware up to date. A compromised router can expose your lock.
