Business Email Compromise (BEC)

⚠️

BEC is the #1 costliest cybercrime for businesses. The FBI reported $2.9 billion in BEC losses in 2023 alone. Attackers impersonate executives, suppliers, or partners to redirect wire transfers or steal credentials.

Business Email Compromise attacks typically follow this pattern: (1) attacker researches the company, (2) spoofs or compromises an executive email, (3) sends urgent wire transfer or credential request, (4) money moves before anyone notices. The solution is always human verification — a quick phone call to the requesting party using a known-good number stops virtually every BEC attack.
